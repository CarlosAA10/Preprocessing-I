Works

General Relativity

Albert Einstein published his work on the General Theory of Relativity in 1915. General Relativity is the geometric theory of gravitation that provides a unified description of gravity to be on a plane of space and time, otherwise known as space-time. Particularly, the curvature on the space-time plane is directly related to the energy and momentum of whatever matter and radiation are present.

Photoelectric effect

in 1905, Einstein published his paper on the Photoelectric Effect. The photoelectric effect was at first a concept put forward by Max Planck, a German physicist, that light waves were tiny bundles or "packets of energy" known as photons or quanta. The photoelectric effect then was known to be the emission of electrons or other free carriers when light hits a material, in the experiments being metal.
